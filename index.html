About Me

Growing up with learning disabilities taught me early that the way something is designed can be the difference between someone succeeding or being left behind. That experience shaped everything — my drive to push through obstacles, my instinct to bridge gaps others walk past, and my deep belief that great design should work for everyone.

Over 15 years at the intersection of design and engineering, I’ve put that belief into practice — most recently at Thermo Fisher Scientific, where I identified a gap between design and development teams, advocated for a new kind of role, and built it into a dedicated UI engineering team from the ground up. I bring fluency in both disciplines: React, TypeScript, Next.js, and Node.js on the engineering side; systems thinking, user research, and accessibility expertise on the design side.

I’m drawn to AI products at the frontier because the design decisions being made right now will shape how millions of people experience technology for the first time. That’s the kind of problem I was built for.
